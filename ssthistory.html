Reasons Why People Came To India

Traders from Central Asia, the Middle East, Southeast Asia, and even Rome came to India for spices, textiles (especially cotton and silk), precious gems, and other goods. India, in return, was a consumer of items like horses, glassware, and fine metals.

Hinduism: India has long been the heart of Hinduism, and people from various parts of Asia (particularly Southeast Asia) traveled to India to visit sacred sites like the Ganges River, the temples of Varanasi, and the Himalayas.

Universities like Nalanda and Takshashila: Ancient Indian centers of learning, such as the universities at Nalanda and Takshashila, attracted scholars, philosophers, and students from distant lands. Notable figures, such as Chinese travelers Fa-Hien and Xuanzang, came to India to study Buddhist scriptures and Indian philosophy.
